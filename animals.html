Indian Elephant

The Indian Elephant is a revered
and iconic species native to India,
known for its majestic presence and
cultural significance.These gentle giants
are essential to India's natural heritage
and play a vital role in the country's
wildlife and traditions.

Indian Sloth Bear

The Indian Sloth Bear, native
to India, is characterized by
a unique shuffling gait and are
known for their primarily insectivorous
diet. They hold a special place in Indian
wildlife,representing a fascinating and
distinctive species found in the country's
forests and wilderness.

Nilgiri Tahr

The Nilgiri Tahr is a mountain
dwelling goat species found in
the Western Ghats and Nilgiri
Hills of South India. It is
known for its striking appearance,
with curved horns and a shaggy coat.
This unique and endangered species is
emblematic of the region's rich
biodiversity and natural beauty.

Indian Pangolin

The Indian Pangolin is a scaly
anteater species native to India.
Covered in protective scales, it is
a nocturnal and insectivorous mammal
known for its unique appearance and
vulnerability due to illegal wildlife trade.
Conservation efforts are crucial
to safeguard this species in India.

Malabar Giant Squirrel

The Malabar Giant Squirrel is a large
and colorful squirrel species found in
the lush forests of the Western Ghats in
India. Its vibrant fur and distinctive
appearance make it a captivating and endemic
wildlife species in this region, where it adds
to the area's rich biodiversity and natural beauty.

Bengal Tiger

The Bengal Tiger, native to the
Indian subcontinent, is the most
numerous tiger subspecies and is known
for its majestic orange coat with dark
stripes.As an iconic symbol of strength
and beauty,it plays a significant role in
India's wildlife and cultural heritage..
